Building a Brain
5th January 2014 | Comments

Happy New Years! Starting off 2014, let's discuss the future of neural simulation. For quite some time now, several projects since the start of the decade have been aimed at recreating the human brain in a virtual environment. The applications would be profound and wide reaching. Firstly, it would go a long way in objectively diagnosing and treating certain types of mental disorders. We could look directly into the brain to see what goes wrong when certain cognitive diseases take hold. We could also work on new drugs that could be virtually tested to see a close approximation of the effects before ever having to do clinical trials. [590 more words]

Intelligence and the Emergence of Strong AI
27th December 2013 | Comments

Why not start my blog off with a post about Artificial Intelligence? Firstly, we need to create a specific definition of intelligence. A perfectly intelligent system for n nodes is, by definition, a system that is able to best take an input, and give the quickest and best desired output, and thus has the smallest latency that is physically possible between nodes, with the least number of errors in communication, with the highest order complexity given the physical properties of the system itself, using the simplest method possible to obtain the highest desired output (or the output with the greatest coherency).
